Introduction to the six books of Muslims

sahih albukharii

Tuesday, May 21, 2024

“Al-Jami’ Al-Musnad Al-Sahih, a summary of the affairs of the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, his Sunnahs and his days,” known as “Sahih Al-Bukhari,” is the most prominent book of the Prophet’s hadith among Muslims of the Sunnis and the community. It was compiled by Imam Muhammad bin Ismail al-Bukhari, and it took him sixteen years to edit it. He selected his hadiths from six hundred thousand hadiths that he collected. The book occupies an advanced position among the Sunnis, as it is one of the six books that are considered among the most important sources of hadith among them, and it is the first book classified in the authentic hadith in its abstract form. It is considered to be the most correct book after the Holy Qur’an. The book of Sahih Al-Bukhari is considered one of the books of the mosques, which contains all the sections of the hadith, including beliefs, rulings, interpretation, history, asceticism, etiquette, and others.

The book gained wide fame during the life of Imam al-Bukhari. It was narrated that more than seventy thousand people heard it from him, and its fame extended to contemporary times and it met with great acceptance and interest from scholars. Many books were written around it, including explanations, summaries, comments, supplements, extracts, and others related to the sciences of hadith, until some historians reported that a number His explanations alone amounted to more than eighty-two explanations.

Sahih Muslim

Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Sahih Muslim is one of the most important books of the Prophet’s Hadith among Sunni Muslims, and they consider it the third most authentic book of all time after the Holy Qur’an and then Sahih al-Bukhari. The book Sahih Muslim is considered one of the comprehensive books, which contains all the chapters of hadith, including beliefs, rulings, etiquette, interpretation, history, virtues, papers, and others.

It was compiled by Abu Al-Hussein Muslim bin Al-Hajjaj Al-Qushayri Al-Naysaburi, and he sought to narrate only the authentic hadiths on which the scholars and hadith scholars had unanimously agreed, so he limited himself to narrating the marfoo’ hadiths and avoided narrating the mu’allaqat and suspended ones and the sayings of the scholars and their jurisprudential opinions, except for what was rare. He spent nearly fifteen years collecting and compiling it, and he compiled it. It contains more than three thousand hadiths without duplicates, and he selected them from three hundred thousand hadiths from his archives.

Sunan Abu Dawud

Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Sunan Abu Dawud, known as Al-Sunan, is one of the six books of hadith and the four Sunnahs, which occupies an advanced position among the Sunnis. It is considered one of the most important sources of hadith among them. Where it comes in rank after the two Sahihs, it was classified by Imam Abu Dawud Suleiman bin Al-Ash’ath bin Ishaq bin Bashir bin Shaddad bin Amr Al-Azdi Al-Sijistani (202 AH - 817 AD / 275 AH - 888 AD), in which Abu Dawud collected a number of hadiths, and his hadiths amounted to 5274 hadiths. He selected it from five hundred thousand hadiths.
Abu Dawud was interested in the hadiths of the rulings that the jurists used as evidence, and on which the Egyptian scholars based the jurisprudential rulings, as he said in his letter to the people of Mecca: “These hadiths are all the hadiths of the Sunnah regarding rulings, but as for many hadiths regarding asceticism and virtues, and other than this, I did not narrate them.” , and he has He divided his book into 36 books, divided each book into 1871 chapters, and translated each hadith according to what the scholars had deduced from it. The book contains hadiths attributed to the Prophet Muhammad, as well as hadiths attributed to the Companions, and traces attributed to the scholars of the Successors.
As for the grading of hadiths, he used to point out extremely weak hadiths, but as for the authentic and good hadiths, he remained silent about them, as he said: “I mentioned the authentic ones and what is similar and close to them, and whatever was very weak I explained, and whatever I did not mention anything in it is valid, and some of them are more authentic than others.” », Hadith scholars differed regarding the hadiths that are silent about them. Ibn al-Salah, Yahya bin Sharaf al-Nawawi, and Ibn Katheer al-Dimashqi see that they are good if they are not in the two Sahihs, and others see that they vary between authentic and good, and some of them are weak and valid for consideration.

Sunan al-Nasa’i

Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Al-Sunan al-Sughra, known as Sunan al-Nasa’i, is one of the six books of hadith compiled by Ahmad bin Shuaib al-Nasa’i, who died in the year 303 AH in the city of Ramla in Palestine. Some Sunni scholars consider it the third of the six books in terms of authenticity.

Introducing Al-Nasa’i

Al-Nasa'i is named after Nasa in Khorasan. He heard from several sheikhs, the most famous of whom are: Ishaq bin Rahawayh and Abu Dawud al-Sijistani. Al-Dhahabi said regarding his right to biographies of noble figures: “No one in the history of 300 had a better memory than Al-Nasa’i. He is more skilled in hadith, its reasons, and its narrators than Muslim, Abu Dawud, and Abu Isa. He is a neighbor at Al-Bukhari and Abu Zara’a track.” Among his works are: the book 'Al-Mujtaba from Al-Sunan Al-Kubra', which is famous for 'Sunan Al-Nasa'i', 'Al-Sunan Al-Kubra', 'The Weak and the Abandoned', and 'The Work of the Day and the Night' in which he mentioned the dhikrs.

Jami' al-Tirmidhi

Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Jami' al-Tirmidhi, known as Sunan al-Tirmidhi, is one of the six books of hadith compiled by Imam al-Tirmidhi. Sunni scholars consider it the fifth of the six books of hadith.
The title of the book Jami’ al-Tirmidhi is: “The brief collection of Sunnahs on the authority of the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, and knowledge of what is correct, what is wrong, and what is to be done.”
Al-Tirmidhi said about this book: “I compiled this book and presented it to the scholars of Hijaz, Iraq, and Khorasan, and they approved of it. Whoever has this book in his house, it is as if there is a prophet speaking in his house.” Judge Abu Bakr Ibn Al-Arabi said in 'Aridah Al-Ahwadhi Sharh Sunan Al-Tirmidhi': “There is nothing in them like the book of Abu Issa, the sweetness of a syllable, the freshness of its purity, and the sweetness of a lawgiver. It contains fourteen knowledge, and that is closer to work and safer: chain of transmission, authentication, weakness, and number of paths.” And wound, and just, and sublime, and conceal, and connect, and cut off, and make clear what is done, and what is abandoned, and he explained the difference of scholars in rejecting and accepting its effects, and mentioned their difference in its interpretation, and each of these sciences is fundamental in its chapter, and is unique in its context, so the reader of it is still in Beautiful gardens and consistent sciences, and this is something that can only be pervaded by abundant knowledge, abundant success, leisure and management.”

Sunan Ibn Majah is one of the books of the Prophet’s hadiths, and it is the sixth of the six books that are the foundations of the honorable Sunnah of the Prophet, by its author, Ibn Majah, Abu Abdullah Muhammad bin Yazid al-Qazwini. His author’s name is his father Yazid, and he is one of the most venerable, greatest, and most enduring of his books over time, and by which he is known and famous. He arranged the hadiths according to Books and chapters, and scholars disagreed about its status among the books of the Sunnah. And the Sunnahs of Ibn Majah include: the authentic, the good, the weak, and even the objectionable and the fabricated ones, although they are few. Whatever the case, the fabricated hadiths are few in comparison to the total number of hadiths in the book, which are more than 4,000 hadiths. He died in the year 273 AH.
